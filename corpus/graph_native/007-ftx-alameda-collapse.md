# FTX, Alameda Research, Binance, and bankruptcy

Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/ftx-founder-indicted-fraud-money-laundering-and-campaign-finance-offenses
Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/samuel-bankman-fried-found-guilty-seven-counts-fraud-and-conspiracy
Source: https://www.reuters.com/technology/crypto-exchange-binance-says-it-will-not-buy-ftx-2022-11-09/

FTX was a cryptocurrency exchange founded by Sam Bankman-Fried. Alameda Research was
a trading firm also associated with Sam Bankman-Fried. In November 2022, concerns
about FTX and Alameda helped trigger customer withdrawals. Binance signed a
non-binding letter of intent to acquire FTX, then backed away after due diligence.
FTX filed for bankruptcy. The United States Department of Justice later charged Sam
Bankman-Fried with fraud and related offenses. A jury found him guilty on seven
counts of fraud and conspiracy in 2023.
