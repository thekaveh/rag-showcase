# Google search antitrust and Apple default search deals

Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-sues-monopolist-google-violating-antitrust-laws
Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-statements-us-v-google-search-antitrust-decision
Source: https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/public-policy/google-search-antitrust-decision/

The United States Department of Justice sued Google in 2020 over search and search
advertising. The case focused on distribution agreements that made Google the
default search engine on browsers and devices. Apple was important because Google
paid to be the default search engine in Safari and on Apple devices. The trial was
heard by Judge Amit Mehta in the United States District Court for the District of
Columbia. In 2024, the court concluded that Google had maintained monopolies in
general search services and general search text advertising. Google disputed the
decision and said it would appeal.
